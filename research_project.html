Research Projects

Title: Secondary Students’ Facial Emotional Expressions during Class

Research Question: This study aims to investigate whether there is any correlation between students’ facial and self-reported emotions. Also, how teachers’ emotions and behaviors are related to students’ facial and self-reported emotions.

Sample: 150 participants from German secondary schools

Result: It is evident from the result that there is a significant positive correlation between students’ facial and reported joy. Also, there is a significant correlation between teachers’ responses and students’ facial joy.

Title: Learning Analytics: Sequence of Questions during Medical History Taking

Research Questions:

  1. To examine the role of questioning items in diagnosing accuracy.
  2. To analyze whether some questions have better predictive power than others.

Sample: 143 medical students took part in the study.

Result: Machine learning models predicted 9 accurately out of 11 predictions with an accuracy of 82%. The findings indicate that the questioning pattern plays a crucial role in arriving at the correct conclusion in diagnostic simulations.

Title: Interest and Effort in Mathematics Affect Math Achievement

Research Question: The purpose of this study was to investigate the effect of interest and effort on math achievement.

Hypothesis: Interest and effort predict math achievement at the concurrent level.

Sample: Data were collected from 1,501 students from grades 5 to 10.

Result: The result showed that interest significantly predicted math achievement, and in later stages, it continued to impact math achievement positively.

Title: The Interrelatedness between Teacher Enjoyment and Teacher-Class Relationship Quality: A Cross-lagged Analysis

Research Question: This study investigated the causal relationship between teacher enjoyment and teacher-student relationship quality over time.

Hypothesis: Teacher enjoyment and teacher-student relationship quality are positively reciprocally linked over measurement times.

Participants: Data were obtained from 93 teachers.

Result: The study showed positive and significant correlations between teacher enjoyment and the teacher-class relationship, especially from T2 to T3. Contrary to the hypothesis, a clear dominance of one cross-lagged path was evident.
